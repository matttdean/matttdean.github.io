Mental Health In America: The Emotional Toll of Chronic Illness

Managing symptoms of a chronic condition is difficult enough, but emotional andmental challenges can also arise. Often, people aren’t just coping with one health issue, but several, and it can have a negative impact on the mental health of patients and their caregivers.

Beginning in 2017, Health Union launched a cross-platform initiative to prioritize mental health content and conversation across all 17 of its online communities.

Mental Health In America​ aimed to improve understanding of mental health concerns among people with chronic health conditions.

They also sought to bring awareness to mental health challenges, validate concerns and connect audience to support their mental health needs.

Improve Understanding

Health Union conducted large-scale surveys across all of its online communities - people living with various chronic health conditions, such as multiple sclerosis, Parkinson’sdisease, lung cancer and many others. We asked questions to understand mental healthchallenges that may result from life with challenging health problems.

“Very frustrating to be fatigued mentally and physically. No energy for everyday routine. Not being able to do these things causes me anxiety.”

“Grief for loss of past life. Depression due to unreliable day to day functionality.”

“My physical limitations hold me back from doing everything that I want to do. In turn that frustrates me sending me down some dark holes.”

Educate, Validate & Connect

Balancing consistency with customization, Health Union created Mental Health In America content and resources across all of its communities to attract and engage people in conversations about mental health. These resources provide relevant information and social support to validate concerns, as well as connect community members through polls, Q&As, and forum discussions.

Educate

Mental Health In America sought to educate audiences about the emotional toll of chronic conditions by featuring a full library of resources and curating mental health content customized to each condition.

Validate

It was critical for people across Health Union's chronic-condition communities to know they’re not alone in experiencing mental health challenges. Personal content - including articles and videos - from patient advocates, Q&As and original illustrations for each article validate both the concerns and experiences of people with these conditions.

Connect

Health Union online communities provided various opportunities for users to connect with Mental Health In America resources - and each other - via social channels, comment sections and mental health-specific discussion forums. Health Union moderators offered support and prompts to encourage conversation.

Making a Difference

Putting a spotlight on the emotional toll of chronic disease generates significant discussions about the effects of mental health challenges, personal experiences and concerns, while enabling people to share personal stories and community members to offer support and encouragement to others.
